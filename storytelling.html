What is Feminist Internet

A place where everyone especially, Queer, women, PWD and LGBTQ+ individuals can access, use, create, and express themselves freely, safely, and equally.

People should be able to make decisions about how they use technology, what data is collected, and how their voices are represented online.

Digital spaces should protect users from harassment, surveillance, and abuse while ensuring privacy, consent, and trust.

A feminist internet includes affordable access, diverse representation, and tools that work for people across languages, abilities, and identities.

#Principle 1: Access

Can everyone get online?

The internet connects people to opportunities, education, healthcare, and communities. Yet not everyone experiences the internet in the same way. Where a person lives often determines the quality of their connection. While urban areas may enjoy fast and reliable internet, many rural communities continue to face slow speeds, unstable networks, and limited digital resources. Access is not simply about being online. It is about having equal opportunities to participate in the digital world.

#Principle 2: Movements

Can communities create change together?

Every day, digital platforms connect voices across boundaries. Movements grow when people move beyond consumption and use digital technology to organize, build networks, and create shared change together. A feminist internet is a powerful tool for social change, encouraging everyone to become builders, innovators, and active participants rather than just passive consumers.

#Principle 3: Economy

Who benefits from technology?

The modern digital economy thrives on user data. Every search, click, and interaction is tracked and monetized by large corporations. Essential information and resources are often hidden behind algorithms or monetized walls. Understanding who controls and profits from these digital systems is a key step toward achieving equity and autonomy online.

#Principle 4

Privacy and Data

Every online action creates data. The websites you visit, the videos you watch, the products you purchase, and even the places you travel all contribute to a digital profile. Privacy is not about hiding information. Privacy is about having control over what information is shared and who can access it.

#Principle 5

Consent

Every day, people click "Accept" without reading lengthy terms and conditions. Many agreements contain hidden permissions that allow platforms to collect, store, and share personal information. True consent requires understanding, transparency, and choice.

#Principle 6

Online Violence

Social media allows people to express themselves, share experiences, and build communities. However, for many users, revealing their identity online can also expose them to discrimination, harassment, and abuse. The internet should be a place where everyone can express themselves without fear.
